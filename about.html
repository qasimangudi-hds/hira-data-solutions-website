Why we started

Big-company rigour, brought down to size

We'd both seen what a serious data function does for a large company — and how rarely a smaller business gets the same. The tools are affordable now; the missing piece is the judgement that turns scattered data into decisions. Hira exists to bring that judgement to businesses that can't yet justify a full data team.

What's in the name

The Cave of Hira

Hira is named after the Cave of Hira — Ghar Hira, in the Mountain of Light near Mecca. It was where the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) would withdraw in solitude: a place of prayer, peace, and reflection, set apart from the noise. And it was there that the angel Jibril (Gabriel) first brought him revelation — where Islam began.

We don't compare ourselves to that. The cave was the birth of something immeasurable; we only hope to be a small beginning — part of a growing movement of Muslim founders building seriously in data and technology here in the UK.

What we carry from it is one idea: clarity. The cave was a place where stepping back made things clear — and that is the whole of our work: helping people pull back from the noise of scattered systems and see, plainly, what their data is telling them, before they act.
